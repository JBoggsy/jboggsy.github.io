James Boggs

I am an AI Research Scientist at the Center for Integrated Cognition, where I work on merging modern computer vision models for object detection, segmentation, and scene reconstruction with the Soar cognitive architecture, a long-standing symbolic framework for intelligent agents. My research sits at the intersection of perception and reasoning: how deep learning-based representations can be harnessed to enrich symbolic cognition and enable agents to build internal world models, ground their knowledge in multimodal experience, and act intelligently over the long term.

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan, advised by John Laird in the Soar Lab. My dissertation, *A Cognitive Architectural Approach to Visual-Symbolic Reasoning*, explored how neuro-symbolic integration can expand the perceptual and reasoning capabilities of cognitive architectures. Along the way, I taught foundational AI courses, mentored motivated students, and discovered that teaching is one of the best ways to deepen your own understanding.

I earned a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.A. in Philosophy from Union College in 2018. During my undergrad, I worked in the Tensegrity Lab under Dr. John Rieffel, where I researched learning gaits for tensegrity robots. I also interned at the Naval Research Laboratory in Washington, D.C., where I worked on rebellious autonomous agents.

My path into AI began less conventionally: as a high school student studying Latin, I wanted to build a translator. That curiosity led me to Python, then NLTK, and eventually to the broader field of AI. In college, I combined my interests in computer science and philosophy, researching tensegrity robotics while also writing on the plausibility of consciousness in artificial intelligences. Those early experiences cemented my fascination with intelligence — both natural and artificial — and continue to shape my work today.

Shortlist — AI-Powered Job Application Tracker

A cross-platform desktop and web application that streamlines the job search process. Shortlist features an AI chat assistant that can search multiple job boards, scrape postings from URLs, and automatically populate a tracking dashboard with structured application data. Users can manage their full hiring pipeline — from application todos and cover letters to interview prep — using a page-based UI with a rich text document editor and switchable AI agent modes (freeform or orchestrated DSPy pipeline). The app supports multiple LLM providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Gemini, or local models via Ollama) through LiteLLM, and packages as native desktop apps for Windows, macOS, and Linux using Tauri.

Current progress: At v0.12.1 with published cross-platform installers. Recent work includes a telemetry system for DSPy optimization, improved resume specialization workflows, and rate-limit handling for job search APIs.

User Environment Simulator

A pip-installable testing and prototyping platform for AI personal assistants. UES provides a web UI and comprehensive REST API (95 endpoints) that simulates a complete user environment across six modalities — email, SMS, calendar, chat, location, and weather — so developers can create realistic, replicable test scenarios for assistant agents. Features an event-sourcing architecture with configurable time control, API key-based authentication, WebSocket and webhook support, multi-agent coordination via a hold system, and a built-in agent testing harness for automated evaluation. Includes a Python client library with sync and async support.

Current progress: At v0.3.0 with all six modalities fully implemented and 3,720 tests passing. Published as a pip-installable package. Agent testing harness, scenario management, and a full Python client library are all functional.

Hopfield Network Explorer

An interactive Jupyter notebook that implements Hopfield networks — a class of recurrent neural networks used as content-addressable (associative) memory — from scratch. Covers the underlying theory of energy functions and Hebbian weight learning, then demonstrates storing, corrupting, and retrieving binary image patterns, with visualizations throughout. A self-contained educational resource.

Current progress: Complete. The notebook covers theory, implementation, and visualization end-to-end and is ready to share as-is.

AIPA — AI Personal Assistant

A proactive AI secretary designed to manage your day. AIPA reads your emails, checks your calendar, monitors the weather, and delivers personalized daily briefings — including wake-up calls via text-to-speech. The system uses a custom agent framework with Jinja2 prompt templating and tool calling for tasks like geolocation, weather retrieval, email management, and smart device control. Designed to integrate with Google APIs (Gmail, Calendar) and the OpenWeather API.

Current progress: Early stage and currently dormant (last updated November 2025). A custom agent framework with tool calling, task management, and prompt templating is implemented, along with basic integrations for email, weather, and geolocation.
