James Boggs

I am a seventh-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan working in the Soar Lab under the advisement of Dr. John Laird. My overarching research interest is on neuro-sybolic artificial intelligence and bridging the gap between low-level perception and high-level cognition. I received an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship in 2018 which funded my first five years of graduate study.

I earned a B.S. in Computer Science and a B.A. in Philosophy from Union College in 2018. During my undergrad, I worked in the Tensegrity Lab under Dr. John Rieffel, where I researched learning gaits for tensegrity robots. I also interned at the Naval Research Laboratory in Washington, D.C., where I worked on rebellious autonomous agents.

Research

I am interested in how we can use deep learning-based feature representations in tandem with symbolic reasoning systems to improve both the perceptual and reasoning capabilities of an agent. Currently, I am working on expanding the Soar cognitive architecture to include visual knowledge and reasoning, including visual representations based on generative models for vision.
